WiFi Nodes

WiFi means that the network will be accessible via your smartphone and will not require any Internet Data. The services currently being demonstrated via the cloud will be mirrored on local servers enabling you to upload and share images, share and remix your work, drop tracks under a 'community mesh license' that aims to protects your copyright whilst promoting freedom. You can also create calenders and backup your diary using BayCal, our WebDav and CalDav server, enabling open-learning and free education.

Suggested method of creating our mesh network:

Repurposed routers, either flashed with OpenWRT, and/or used as base stations for Raspberry Pi servers.

For various reason, many of the old routers in South Africa will not be able to be flashed, but may be repurposed into our project. We will carry information on which hardware works as we move forward on our wiki. The goal is to either piggyback Raspberry Pi Zeros into the old boxes, or even create our own new boxes via 3D printing ABS or lazer cut plywood.

Currently most routers have a USB port and may be setup to act as nodes.

Nodes more Technical

The project is currently exploring various methods of generating mesh nodes, including deploying LibreMesh which runs on the Batman protocol, or using the Babel Mesh Routing protocol, or returning FFRouting to its origins as Quagga, named after an extinct Zebra once found on the slopes of Table Mountain. We will also be experimenting with Virtual Public Network (VPuN) relays and even exit gateways.

The Tinc project provides a means of creating a VPN with meshing, as does Meshbird. Other contenders, are n2n and fastd.

Connectivity

For obvious reasons, connectivity to the network will essentially be gratis to avoid having to apply for an ISP license. We do not intend to become an ISP, but merely to offer a Community LAN / Mesh experience.

If the community want to provide an alternative service, the suggested route is to open your router access to the general public or deploy a virtual gateway such as Althea.

Explore solutions such as Bridge to the Net

We plan to offer various edge-hosted services, including Freedom Box

Open Collective

Setting up as an opencollective or affiliating to an opencollective host is the preferred means of creating organisational transparency whilst enabling the necessary fund-raising ability. Any ideas on this step would be appreciated. If you wish to become a supporter, donate, or give of your time, please feel free. The preferred method of support is donations running forward. You are free to pay a monthly donation of whatever you can afford, anywhere from R20-R100 per month, to cover our infrastructure costs, which though low, still cost money. Please see Ko-Fi button, or you can just drop a PayPal Me

Once we get more organised and collectively, decentralised, there will be an AGM and a NPO/CBO in which you may also participate.

Membership

Currently membership is restricted to the residents of Muizenberg and also interested parties. In future the community will set its criteria for federating with other community projects for example iNethi in Ocean View. We aim to be as decentralised as possible while promoting an urban commons. A single-sign-on SSO solution is being evaluated and will enable members and users to sign-on from a single page and authorise with all the apps available via our network.

Go ahead sign-up to our chat server powered by rocketchat. We still need to build our onboarding system, so please be patient. The system is currently in Alpha Preview mode.
